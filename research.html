Adaptive Governance Meta-Models for Generative AI Advisory Systems

My master's thesis and PhD proposal investigate how generative-AI advisory systems can learn from execution evidence without allowing probabilistic AI to replace deterministic accountability.

The core design problem is governed adaptation: recommendations should improve over time, but rule changes must remain traceable, reviewable, and constrained by explicit governance invariants.

The research is demonstrated through microenterprise marketing advisory, where small business owners need recommendations that are not only plausible, but feasible under real constraints such as time, budget, trust, language, and execution capacity.

Related artifact: LuxPilot.

The Cultural Unconscious of Language Models

This working research stream asks whether language models of different cultural-linguistic origin show systematic differences in implicit symbolic production: narrative completions, projective responses, word associations, symbol interpretation, and serial-reproduction drift.

The study design separates model origin from prompt language through a crossed origin × language design and compares implicit-symbolic outputs with explicit value instruments.

Status: study design and pilot protocol

Temporal Experience Indicators for AI Consciousness Assessment

This working manuscript proposes a theory-grounded indicator battery for assessing temporal-experience properties in AI systems.

The goal is to distinguish temporal reasoning from temporal experience: knowing facts about time is not the same as having an internally structured temporal present, retention, protention, felt duration, temporal self-location, or diachronic binding.

Status: working manuscript

Research Positioning

Across these projects, my focus is not on treating LLMs as magical agents, but on designing systems and instruments that make their behavior measurable, bounded, and accountable.
