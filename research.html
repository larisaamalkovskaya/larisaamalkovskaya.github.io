Research

Governance, adoption, and assessment of AI systems.

My research sits where AI capability meets accountability: how advisory systems can stay governable as they grow more capable, and how we should assess properties like temporal experience in AI systems. It runs alongside my graduate work at the University of Luxembourg and my own AI products.

Papers & manuscripts

Temporal Experience Indicators for AI Consciousness Assessment: Knowing About Time Is Not Being In Time

Preprint, July 2026 (sole author). A theory-grounded indicator battery that separates temporal reasoning from temporal experience in AI systems: six temporal-experience indicators derived from the phenomenology of inner time, applied across four architecture classes, for strictly necessity-side use — the absence of these indicators lowers credence in consciousness attributions, while their presence does not establish it. Journal submission in preparation (Neuroscience of Consciousness).

The Governance Paradox: Why Governance Must Scale with Capability in Generative-AI Advisory Systems

Argues that as generative-AI capability grows, governance must scale with it rather than retreat. Develops the design principle at the core of a governed advisory architecture. Manuscript in preparation.

The Cultural Unconscious of Language Models

A study design for an implicit symbolic battery comparing model-origin effects across narrative completions, word associations, symbol interpretations, and serial-reproduction drift. Work in progress.
